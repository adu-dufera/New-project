Coronaviruses are a large family of viruses, some of which causes illness in people, and others that circulate among mammals and birds. Rarely,animal coronaviruses can spread to humans, and then spread between people. Zoonotic coronaviruses have emerged in recent years to cause human outbreaks such as COVID-19.

Major Symptoms

Symptoms in general usually begin 2 to 14 days after you come into contact with the virus.Eventhough some people are infected but don't notice any symptoms, most people will have MILD symptoms and get better on their own.

HIGH FEVER.

Varying degrees of fever that can persist for days even weeks.

DRY-COUGH

Dry and persistent cough, accompanied with shortness of breath.

Difficulty Breathing

Difficulty breathing accompanied by chest tightness.

Minor Symptoms

RUNNY-NOSE

DIARRHEA

ACHES

In severe cases patients might exhibit shortness of breath or difficulty breathing, pneumonia, persistent chest pain, and it might even lead to death.

Mode of transmision

Droplet Transmission

Occurs when a person is in close contact (within 1m)with someone who has respiratory symptoms e.g coughing or sneezing and is therefore at risk of having his/her mucosae (mouth& nose) or conjuctiva (eyes) exposed to potentially infective respiratory droplets.

Contact Transmission

There's one other route that's thought to play a role in the spread of COVID-19; contact transmission. In that situation viral particles emitted from the respiratory tract of an infected individual land on a surface. Then, another person touches that object,then touches their nose, mouth or eyes. The virus then sneaks into the body via the mucous membranes, infecting the second person.

Who is more at risk

Older Age

These are older people(that is people over 60years old).The risk of severe disease gradually increases with age starting from around 40 yeards. Its important that adults also should protect themselves and in turn protect others that may be more vulnerable.

Underlying Diseases

Those with underlying medical conditions (such as Cardiovascular Diseases, ASTHMA, DIABETES, cancer and other Chronic respiratory diseases) are much more vulnerable.

How to Prevent getting Corona

Clean your hands often.Use soap and water,or an alcohol-based hand rub.

Maintain a safe distance from anyone who is coughing or sneezing.

Cover your mouth and nose with your bent elbow or a tissue when you cough os sneeze.

Follow the directions of your loacl health authority.
